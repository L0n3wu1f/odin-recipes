Mochi

Description

Mochi is a type of Japanese dessert consisting of a sweet red bean paste cakes with a glutinous flour outer layer called mochigome. The texture is soft and chewy, and is often flavored with different types of powders such as matcha. Mochi can be made with food colorings to give it a more presentable look.

Mochi consists mainly of red bean paste, glutinous rice flour, green tea powder(matcha), water, sugar, and cornstarch for consistency.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup sweetened red bean paste
  • 1 cup glutinous rice flour
  • 1 teaspoon matcha
  • 1 cup water
  • 1/4 cup white sugar
  • 1/2 cup cornstarch

Steps

  1. Wrap red bean paste in aluminum foil and place in the freezer until solid, at least 1 hour.
  2. Mix rice flour and matcha in a microwavable bowl. Stir in water and sugar; mix until smooth.
  3. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and microwave for 3.5 minutes.
  4. Remove bean paste from the freezer and divide into 8 equal balls and set aside.
  5. Remove rice flour mixture from the microwave. Stir and heat, covered, for another 15 to 30 seconds.
  6. Dust a work area with the cornstarch. Roll about 2 tablespoons of hot rice flour mixture into a ball. Flatten the ball and place one ball of frozen bean paste in the center. Pinch and press the dough around the bean paste until completely covered. Sprinkle with additional cornstarch and place mochi, seam-side down, in a paper muffin liner to prevent sticking.
  7. Repeat previous step for remaining mochi.
